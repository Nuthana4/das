About Vaagdevi College of Engineering

Vaagdevi College of Engineering (VCE), located in Bollikunta, Warangal, is a leading institute under the Viswambhara Educational Society. The college is known for quality engineering education, discipline, and a student-centric learning environment.

  • Affiliation: JNTU Hyderabad (JNTUH)
  • Facilities: Smart classrooms, advanced laboratories, digital library, innovation centers
  • Industry Connect: Workshops, internships, technical fests & MoUs with companies
  • Placements: Strong training & placement cell with top recruiter collaborations

About the Data Science Branch

Data Science focuses on extracting insights from data using Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Statistics, and Programming. It prepares students for practical, industry-ready roles across many domains.

  • Core Skills: Python, SQL, ML, AI, Deep Learning
  • Careers: Data Scientist, ML Engineer, Analyst, AI Developer
  • Why DS? High demand, global opportunities, and strong career growth

About DaS

Why use the DaS Website?

The DaS website centralizes academic and community resources so students can find what they need quickly and reliably. It supports learning, collaboration, and campus engagement by providing:

  • Organized curriculum: Year- and semester-wise subject listings and up-to-date syllabus copies.
  • Exam preparation: Previous year question papers and curated PDF notes for focused study.
  • Learning resources: Curated MOOCs, datasets, and recommended readings to supplement coursework.
  • Community & clubs: Central place to discover clubs (Data Quest, Caring Hands) and join activities.
  • Events & opportunities: Announcements for hackathons, symposiums, E-Summit, and campus celebrations.
  • Mentorship & industry links: Guest lectures, internship notices, and alumni connections to bridge academics with industry.

Data Quest Club

The Data Quest Club is the technical arm of DaS for students who want hands-on experience building data-driven solutions. It focuses on practical skill-building, collaboration, and preparing students for competitions and industry projects.

  • Activities: weekly workshops, project sprints, Kaggle & datathon teams, code reviews, and guest speaker sessions.
  • Membership: open to all levels — beginners to advanced; mentorship circles pair newcomers with senior students.
  • Representative Projects: recommendation systems, image-classification pilots, campus analytics dashboards.
  • Join: attend a meetup or contact the club lead via the department office.

Caring Hands Club

Caring Hands is our community and outreach club focused on social impact. It brings together students who want to contribute to society through volunteer work, awareness campaigns, and collaborative service projects.

  • Activities: blood drives, community tutoring, environmental clean-ups, fundraisers, and health awareness events.
  • Membership: all students are welcome — activities scheduled flexibly around academics.
  • Impact: partnerships with local NGOs and campus initiatives that provide measurable community benefits.
  • Join: attend the next introductory meeting or contact the club coordinator.

Syllabus Box

  • Year-wise Subjects: Clear lists of subjects and course objectives for each academic year/semester.
  • Previous Year Question Papers: Downloadable past papers organized by year and subject to aid exam preparation.

Events & Celebrations

  • P4P: Flagship project showcase where student teams present practical, impact-focused projects for campus and community use — encourages hands-on learning and cross-team collaboration.
  • Teachers' Day Celebration: Annual event to honor faculty, featuring student performances, guest talks, and award recognitions.
  • Ganesh Chaturthi: Community festival with cultural programs, group prayers, and student-led volunteer activities that foster unity and celebration on campus.

Which Is Better?

Both clubs offer valuable experiences — the right choice depends on what you want to gain:

  • Data Quest Club: Best if you want technical training, project experience, and competition prep.
  • Caring Hands Club: Best if you seek community service, leadership in outreach, and social impact work.
  • Try both: Many students join both clubs to build technical skills while contributing to the community — complementary benefits.
  • How to choose: Attend intro meetings for each club, try a workshop, then decide which aligns with your goals.
